About

We are Project Reboot, the 2021 hackathon run by the Wayland High School Computer Science Club. Our goal is to show that coding is fun for all people, regardless of skill level, through creating Hackathons that help those in need.
For example, last year's Hackathon brought Wayland and many towns in the Greater Boston Area together by developing tools and applications to help blind users. This year, we want to bring the region's towns together once again by working together to improve the quality of remote learning.

FAQ

What is Project Reboot?

It's a one-day hackathon for all high school students in the Greater Boston area. Students work in teams to build a prototype of an app, game, web site or device that tackles accessibility issues. Using design thinking, students learn how to develop new ideas and design technology that can make life better for people with disabilities.

Who can participate?

Any high school student in grades 9-12 who's interested in coding, digital media, exploring AI and other technologies, helping a billion people, or entrepreneurship can attend. If you've graduated from high school, you can apply to be a mentor. If you're in middle school, we'll have to see. Please contact us for more information.
